⭐ Must-See Sightseeing

🏆 Top 5 — Don't Miss These

🏰 Prague Castle + St. Vitus Cathedral MUST
📍 Hradčany — west bank hilltop
World's largest ancient castle complex. St. Vitus Cathedral is jaw-dropping inside. Don't skip the Golden Lane (tiny colorful houses, Kafka lived in #22).
💡 Go in the morning to avoid crowds. Free to walk the grounds; ticket needed for interiors (~250 CZK).
🌉 Charles Bridge (Karlův most) MUST
📍 Connects Old Town ↔ Malá Strana
Iconic 14th-century bridge with 30 baroque statues. Best crossed early morning or at sunset for photos. Street musicians, artists, incredible views.
💡 Walk it at sunrise (~6:30 AM) for an almost empty bridge + golden light.
🕰️ Old Town Square + Astronomical Clock MUST
📍 Staré Město — city center
Medieval square with the famous clock (every hour it does a little show with apostle figures), Týn Church, and colorful baroque buildings.
💡 Watch the clock strike the hour, but don't eat at the restaurants here — tourist trap prices!
🌿 Letná Park + Beer Garden MUST
📍 Letná — north of the river
THE best panoramic view of Prague — all the bridges, the castle, the red rooftops. Giant metronome where a Stalin statue used to stand. Amazing beer garden.
💡 Perfect afternoon spot. Grab a beer (50 CZK) and enjoy the view.
🏰 Vyšehrad MUST
📍 South of city center — above the Vltava
Ancient fortress with incredible views, much quieter than Prague Castle. Cemetery where Dvořák and other Czech legends are buried. Beautiful basilica.
💡 Perfect for Saturday morning before heading to Děčín. Free entry to the grounds!

👍 Also Great If You Have Time

✡️ Jewish Quarter (Josefov) WORTH IT
📍 Next to Old Town
Old Jewish Cemetery (hauntingly beautiful), synagogues, Franz Kafka statue. Compact — takes about 1-2 hours.
🗼 Žižkov TV Tower WORTH IT
📍 Žižkov
Love it or hate it — a brutalist tower with giant crawling baby sculptures by David Černý. Observation deck + sky bar at the top.
🎭 Dancing House PHOTO STOP
📍 Nové Město — along the river
Gehry-designed "Fred & Ginger" building. Rooftop bar with views. Quick photo stop, 5 min from Náplavka.
📖 Strahov Library HIDDEN GEM
📍 Near Prague Castle
Two stunning baroque library halls — looks like something from a movie. Also has a monastery brewery!
🌊 Náplavka Riverbank CHILL
📍 Along the Vltava — near your hotel!
Prague's favorite hangout along the river. Weekend farmers market, food boats, and drinks on the water.
🍽️ Czech Food — What to Try

Czech cuisine is hearty, meaty, and beer-friendly. Here's what's actually good vs. tourist bait:

⭐ Must-Try Dishes

🥩 Svíčková na smetaně
THE National Dish
Marinated beef sirloin in creamy vegetable sauce with bread dumplings (knedlíky) and cranberry sauce. Comfort food perfection.
🍖 Vepřo-knedlo-zelo
Classic Czech
Roast pork with dumplings and sauerkraut. Simple but incredibly satisfying. Every Czech grandma's recipe.
🧀 Smažený sýr
Czech Comfort Food
Deep-fried cheese (usually Edam) with tartar sauce and fries. It's basically a cheese schnitzel. Street food classic.
🍺 Czech Beer (Pivo)
Religion, basically
Czechs drink more beer per capita than anyone. Try Pilsner Urquell, Kozel, Staropramen — but always from the TAP (tankové pivo), never bottled.
🥟 Knedlíky (Dumplings)
Side Dish Essential
Bread dumplings or potato dumplings — served with everything. The sweet version (ovocné knedlíky) filled with fruit is amazing as dessert.
🍯 Medovník
Dessert
Czech honey cake — layers of honey sponge with cream. Delicate and not too sweet. Find it in any traditional café.

⚠️ Tourist Traps to Avoid

🥨 Trdelník
Tourist Trap!
Those chimney cake things everywhere? They're actually Hungarian, not Czech at all. Locals don't eat them. Skip it.
🍽️ Old Town Square restaurants
Avoid
Overpriced, mediocre food. Walk 5 minutes in any direction for half the price and double the quality.

📍 Where to Eat

🍺 Lokál (Dlouhá st.)
Best tankové pivo (tank beer) in Prague. Authentic Czech food, packed with locals. Reservations recommended!
🍷 Vinohrady district
Walk around Jiřího z Poděbrad square — tons of great restaurants, wine bars, and craft beer spots. Best food neighborhood.
🎸 Žižkov district
Highest bar density in Prague. Alternative vibes, cheap beers, authentic pubs. Great for a bar crawl.
🌊 Náplavka food boats
Food boats along the river near your hotel. Weekend market with local food, craft drinks.
📖 Prague — A City Through the Ages

Every building you see tells a story. Here's what shaped the city you're walking through.

🪨 Origins (8th–10th Century)

Slavic tribes settled where the Vltava had a natural ford. Prague Castle was founded around 880 AD on the hilltop — it's been the seat of power for over 1,100 years. Vyšehrad fortress rose on the opposite cliff as a rival power base.

🏛️ What you'll see

Romanesque rotundas — tiny round churches are Prague's oldest surviving buildings. Look for the Rotunda of St. Martin at Vyšehrad (~1100 AD) and St. Cross Rotunda in the Old Town.

👑 Golden Age — Emperor Charles IV (1346–1378)

The defining era. Charles IV made Prague the capital of the Holy Roman Empire — briefly the most powerful city in Europe. He was obsessed with making Prague magnificent:

  • Charles Bridge (1357) — 16 arches, 30 baroque statues. Foundation stone laid at exactly 5:31 AM on July 9 (chosen by astrologers!)
  • St. Vitus Cathedral — construction started 1344, took 600 years to finish (!)
  • Charles University (1348) — oldest university in Central Europe
  • New Town (Nové Město) — planned city expansion, revolutionary urban design for the era

Prague became the 3rd largest city in Europe after Rome and Constantinople.

🏛️ What you'll see

Gothic architecture everywhere. The pointed arches, ribbed vaults, flying buttresses of St. Vitus Cathedral. The Charles Bridge towers. The powder tower (Prašná brána) near Old Town. Gothic = Charles IV's era.

⚔️ Hussites & The Defenestrations (1419–1620)

Jan Hus, a Czech reformer, was burned at the stake in 1415 — a full century before Luther. This triggered the Hussite Wars. Prague became a hotbed of religious rebellion.

Then in 1618: the famous Defenestration of Prague — Protestant nobles threw two Catholic governors out of a castle window (they survived, landing in a dung heap). This started the Thirty Years' War that devastated all of Europe.

🏛️ What you'll see

The window at Prague Castle where it happened (marked with a plaque). The Týn Church on Old Town Square was a Hussite stronghold. Bethlehem Chapel where Hus preached.

🏛️ Habsburg Baroque (17th–18th Century)

After the Battle of White Mountain (1620), the Habsburgs re-Catholicized Bohemia and rebuilt Prague in extravagant Baroque style. Italian and Austrian architects transformed the city. This is why Malá Strana looks the way it does — pure Baroque magic.

  • St. Nicholas Church (Malá Strana) — one of Europe's finest Baroque churches
  • Wallenstein Palace — first Baroque palace in Prague, enormous gardens
  • Clementinum — Jesuit complex with the stunning Baroque library (the one that looks like a movie set!)
  • Loreto — Baroque pilgrimage destination near the Castle
🏛️ What you'll see

Baroque = curves, gold, drama. Onion domes, ornate facades, theatrical interiors. Most of Malá Strana, the Castle gardens, and the statues on Charles Bridge (added 1683–1714) are Baroque. If it looks like a wedding cake, it's Baroque.

✡️ Jewish Prague (10th Century–WWII)

One of the oldest continuous Jewish communities in Europe. The Prague ghetto (Josefov) was both a place of persecution and extraordinary cultural achievement.

  • Old Jewish Cemetery (1439–1787) — 12 layers of graves stacked on top of each other because the community wasn't allowed to expand. Hauntingly beautiful.
  • Old-New Synagogue (1270) — oldest active synagogue in Europe! Legend says the Golem of Prague is hidden in its attic.
  • In 1850, the ghetto was renamed "Josefov" after Emperor Joseph II who granted Jews civil rights
  • The Nazis paradoxically preserved Jewish artifacts here — they planned a "Museum of an Extinct Race." The community survived, and the museum became a memorial instead.
🏛️ What you'll see

Six synagogues, the Old Cemetery, and the Franz Kafka statue (he was born here in 1883). The Art Nouveau buildings around Josefov were built in the 1890s when the medieval ghetto was demolished and rebuilt.

🎨 Art Nouveau & National Revival (19th Century)

Czech nationalism blossomed. The language was revived, national institutions built, and Alphonse Mucha defined Art Nouveau worldwide — he was Czech!

  • National Theatre (1881) — funded entirely by public donations, a symbol of Czech identity
  • National Museum — dominates the top of Wenceslas Square
  • Municipal House (Obecní dům) — the most spectacular Art Nouveau building in Prague. Café, concert hall, Mucha murals inside.
  • Prague Main Station (Praha hl.n.) — where you arrive! Art Nouveau interior hidden under the communist-era concrete shell
🏛️ What you'll see

Art Nouveau = flowing lines, floral motifs, elegant curves. Look UP at building facades along the Old Town, especially around Wenceslas Square and near the Powder Tower. The Municipal House café is a must-stop.

🕊️ 20th Century — Drama Upon Drama

  • 1918: Czechoslovakia declared independent. Prague becomes capital. Democracy under Masaryk.
  • 1938-39: Munich Agreement → Nazi occupation. Czech resistance, Heydrich assassination (1942).
  • 1945: Liberation. Then communist takeover in 1948.
  • 1968: Prague Spring — reformist Dubček tries to create "Socialism with a human face." Soviet tanks roll in on August 21. Crushed. (Wenceslas Square was the center of resistance.)
  • 1989: Velvet Revolution — peaceful mass protests. Václav Havel, a playwright, becomes president. Communism falls without a single shot.
  • 1993: Czechoslovakia peacefully splits into Czech Republic and Slovakia ("Velvet Divorce").
🏛️ What you'll see

Wenceslas Square: where history happened — 1918 independence, 1968 tanks, 1989 revolution. The Žižkov TV Tower (brutalist, communist era) with David Černý's crawling babies. The Metronome at Letná replaced the world's largest Stalin statue (dynamited in 1962). Dancing House (1996, Gehry) symbolizes the new, free Prague.

🧠 Spot the Architectural Styles — Quick Cheat Sheet

Renaissance (1500-1600)Symmetry, columns, sgraffito (scratched plaster patterns). Baroque (1600-1750)Curves, gold, drama, onion domes. Malá Strana. Art Nouveau (1890-1910)Flowing lines, flowers, elegant. Municipal House. Cubism (1910-1920)Angular facades — Prague has the ONLY cubist buildings in the world! Brutalist (1960-1989)Concrete blocks. 🥾 Bohemian Switzerland — Hiking

Base: Hotel Mezní Louka, right in the heart of the national park.

⭐ Pravčická Gate (Prebischtor)
📏 ~6 km roundtrip • ⏱ 2-3h • 🏔️ Europe's largest natural sandstone arch!
From Mezní Louka, take Gabriela's Trail (~3 km) directly to the gate. THE highlight of the region. Go early morning for fewer crowds and best light.
🛶 Edmund Gorge (Wild Gorge)
📏 ~4 km one way from Hřensko • ⏱ 1-2h • Boat ride through narrow cliffs!
Spectacular gondola ride through towering sandstone walls. Combine: Prebischtor morning → Gorge afternoon. The combo is the classic day trip.
🌲 Jetřichovice Viewpoints
📏 ~8 km loop • ⏱ 3-4h • Less touristy, stunning formations
Mariina Viewpoint, Vilemínina Stěna — beautiful rock pillars and forest trails. Hřebenovka trail is the scenic route. Quieter than the gorge circuit.
🌡️ Weather Note

Early April: expect 5–12°C. Bring layers + waterproof jacket. Trails can be muddy. Sturdy shoes essential!

💡 Pronunciation Tips

• č = "ch" as in "church"
• š = "sh" as in "shoe"
• ž = "zh" as in "measure"
• ř = uniquely Czech! Like "rž" — rolled R + zh combined
• ě = "yeh"
• Stress is ALWAYS on the first syllable!

💡 Practical Info

🚇 Getting Around Prague

  • Metro + Tram — Excellent network. Ticket via PID Lítačka app or machines at stations
  • 30-min ticket: 30 CZK (~€1.20) • 24h ticket: 120 CZK (~€4.80)
  • From main station → Mama Shelter: Metro C (red) → Smíchovské nádraží, or tram
  • Walking: Central Prague is very walkable — most sights within 30 min of each other

💰 Money & Prices

  • Currency: Czech Koruna (CZK) — €1 ≈ 25 CZK
  • Cards: Accepted almost everywhere. Still bring some cash for small shops/markets
  • Beer: 50–70 CZK (€2–3) 🍺 • Meal: 200–400 CZK (€8–16)
⚠️ Exchange Office Scam

"0% Commission" does NOT mean good rate! Always use a bank ATM with your own card. When the ATM asks "convert to EUR?" → always choose NO (pay in CZK). Exchange offices near tourist spots are notorious rip-offs.
